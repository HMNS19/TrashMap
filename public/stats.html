Waste Management in India

Current Situation

India produces 62 million tons of waste annually, growing at 4% per year. By 2030, municipal solid waste is expected to rise to 165 million tons due to rapid urbanization.

Challenges

  • Rapid Urbanization: Only 43 million tons of the 62 million tons of waste produced yearly are collected, with the rest dumped untreated.
  • Poor Infrastructure: Insufficient waste collection, with only 21 million garbage collectors.
  • Recycling Issues: Only 30% of waste is properly sorted, leading valuable materials to end up in landfills.

Solutions

  • Investment in Technology: Improve waste collection and recycling infrastructure, and use innovative technologies like GPS tracking.
  • Integration of Sectors: Combine informal and formal waste management, and promote decentralized waste management by communities.
  • Treatment Innovations: Encourage composting and waste-to-energy methods, while enforcing strict waste management rules.
